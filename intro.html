So I'm hardcoding this site. I've developed web apps for decades from Cold Fusion to Ruby on Rails, PHP, and Django, but here we are. A full on retro site minus the cool css template and bootstrap. I don't believe I can run a DB backend on github, which is why I'm doing this. I am using VI nonetheless just to add to the pain. I'm working on IOT stuff because Wink went to a pay model, so my next post will hopefully be on what I did to ditch wink.

Ditching Wink

I purchased a wink hub a long time ago (2012 I think?) and it has worked for a handful of lights that are Zigbee or Zwave. I prefer putting these lights on these networks becuase they don't clutter my Wifi (I have over 40 devices on my home network), and I think it is nice to have them separated from my network. I also know that the range can be better as well. However Wink has decided to pull out a subscription model. Ugh! What? Here is the original post:

Should you choose not to sign up for a subscription you will no longer be able to access your Wink devices from the app, with voice control or through the API, and your automations will be disabled on May 13. Your device connections, settings and automations can be reactivated if you decide to subscribe at a later date.

So I did what any comptuer scientist does--research how I can just run the service myself. I began by purchasing an Aeotec Z5 stick. Worked well for my Zwave lights, but it turns out wink GE lights are zigbee. Darn. Well then I picked up a raspbee, and put that on the raspberry Pi that i used for the Zstick. Now I have all the technical parts to replace wink. One by one I added lights and now I am Wink free! I am using domticz service, raspicon connected to domiticz, and I can control it all. I even bought a few more lights out of spite of Wink. I got an RGB light and a Cree light, and they all integreated right in. Woot!

Bye Bye Wink! You served me well, but it is time to move on.
